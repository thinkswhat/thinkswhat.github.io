Hello and welcome to my homepage! My name is Jianqi Yang, 杨建祺 in Chinese.

I received my undergraduate education at the intersection of data science, electromechanical engineering, and management science, utilising these skills to explore various areas within environmental engineering and atmospheric sciences to identify my true interests. I am particularly excited about the convergence of computational technology, data science, and atmospheric sciences, including developing diffusion models based on computational fluid dynamics and atmospheric dynamics, analysing Earth system observation data using machine learning and statistical methods, and creating new algorithms and models to address issues of incomplete and unbalanced observational data. Additionally, I am exploring the application of edge machine learning models for measurement. I devoted most of my spare time during the final years of my undergraduate studies to deepening my understanding of this field and participating in several highly enjoyable and challenging research projects.

I am currently conducting research as a visiting student in the [ACCESS Lab](https://atmosphere-westlake.com/research.html) at [Westlake University](https://en.westlake.edu.cn), where my main focus is on developing various analysis and prediction models suitable for Earth system observations to fully exploit incomplete and unbalanced observational data, gaining new insights into urban atmospheric systems. This exploratory work involves models based on multiple principles, including mechanistic models, data-driven models, and models incorporating techniques such as data assimilation.

I am actively seeking PhD opportunities to take the next step in this exciting field, which both pushes the boundaries of human knowledge and applies new technologies for the benefit of humanity.
